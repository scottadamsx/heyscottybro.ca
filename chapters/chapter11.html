Chapter 11: Sparks

The fuse was always lit. We just got better at pretending we couldn’t smell the smoke.

Coming home hadn’t changed anything. If anything, it made things worse. I was bigger now. Stronger. Louder. Sharper around the edges. So was he. But only on the inside. The difference was—I wasn’t afraid to show mine anymore.

We’d been dancing around each other for weeks. Tight hallways. Muted greetings. Passive-aggressive silences passed like heirlooms. Then one night, it broke.

It was small.

It always is.

A comment. A tone. A misunderstanding thick with years of unresolved shit behind it. We were in the kitchen. I said something about dinner. He snapped. “You don’t like it, don’t eat it.” I pushed back. “It’s not about the food.” “Then what’s it about, tough guy?”

There it was. That tone. The one that said you’re still a boy to me.

Sophie was in the kitchen. So was Mom. Abbey was out. I don’t even remember the rest of what we said. It spiraled. Fast. Sophie said my name once. I ignored her. Then I said I was leaving. Taking the car.

He stepped in front of me. Blocked the door. “You don’t take what you don’t earn.” He snatched the keys out of my hand.

And that’s when it happened. Not a punch. Not a slap. But we were shoving. Grabbing. Furniture shifted. Chairs scraped tile. My body moved on instinct. His did too. Two lions in a room too small for one.

Sophie screamed. Mom jumped in between us, yelling. “Sophie—call the police!”

Sophie froze. Her back hit the counter. She sank to the floor, breathing like she was underwater. Panic attack. Mom was pushing me. Pushing him. Screaming both our names.

And then—

Silence.

I stepped back. Looked down. He was on the floor. I was standing over him. Mom had her hand on my chest, holding me back even though I wasn’t moving anymore. Sophie was sobbing into her hands. The kitchen smelled like sweat, fear, and burned pride.

I wasn’t afraid of him anymore. That terrified me most.
